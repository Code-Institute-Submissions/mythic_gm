What is a roleplaying game?


Table top Role playing games (ttrpg) such as Dungeons & Dragons and Call of Cthulhu are pen & paper, collaborative story telling games that uses dice to determine results.


In most roleplaying games, there are two distinct roles. The Game Master(GM) and the players.


The GM is the main storyteller for the game. They create and describe the adventure for the players, introduce conflicts, control any non-playable characters(NPC) the players interact with, give the players goals to accomplish, and describe the outcomes of dice rolls.


The players each control one character that they can create themselves. They work together to overcome any conflicts and plotlines introduced by the GM. They do this by describing what they want to do and rolling dice to see if they were successful or not.


In most games, player actions are limited by the choices given to them by the designer of the game. Role-playing games are different in that the players have complete control over where they want to go and what they want to do.





Mythic


Mythic is a tool for use with your table-top RPG sessions. It is a simple GM emulator. You can ask it yes/no questions and generate random events that focus on any relevant NPC and story threads in your adventure. It is a digital implimentation of the Mythic Game Master Emulator book.


Normally, before a session begins, the GM must spend a lot of time generating the adventure, coming up with plot points, characters and obstacles.

Mythic is different in that it requires no preparation from the GM. Mythic adventures are meant to be played off the cuff, with perhaps a few minutes of brainstorming to come up with the initial setup.


Mythic can also be played entirely without a GM. The same mechanics in Mythic that allow a GM to run an adventure without preparation also allows a group of players to play without a GM.


The possibilities of what mythic can generate are limited only by the players imagination. At times answers will be very clear, and at other times they will be vauge and require a good bit of interpritation.


If you don't know anything about role playing games, thats ok. Mythic can be used as a creative writing tool to generate ideas for stories, characters and plot twists. Any author can use it for insperation when they have writers block.

How to use this with another gaming system?


Mythic provides structure and ideas for your adventure to unfold naturally, but what about rules for fighting or skill checks?


Each gaming system will have its own rules for combat, for chases, for skill checks. If you get caught in a confrontation playing a game of Dungeons and Dragons, then use these rules for resolving the combat.


You can also just use mythic and question fate to resolve anything you want. You can ask fate " I punch the zombie, does it die?" set realistic odds for your character and see what happens.
